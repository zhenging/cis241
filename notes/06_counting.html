6.2 The Pigeonhole Principle

Ex: 1-3, 5-7
HW 2, 6, 9

Theorem 1 - The pigeonhole principle

If k is a positive integer and k+1 or more objects are placed into kkk boxes, then there is at least one box containing two or more of the objects.

Theorem 2 - Generalized pigeonhole principle

If NNN object are placed into kkk boxes, then there is at least one box containing ⌈N/k⌉\lceil N/k \rceil⌈N/k⌉ objects.

  • Permutation (order matters)

If nnn is positive integer and rrr is an integer with 0⩽r⩽n0 \leqslant r \leqslant n0⩽r⩽n, then there are P(n,r)=n(n−1)⋯(n−r+1)=n!(n−r)!P(n, r) = n(n-1) \cdots (n-r+1) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}P(n,r)=n(n−1)⋯(n−r+1)=(n−r)!n!​

  • Combination (order does not matter)

The number is rrr-combinations of a set with nnn elements, where nnn is a nonnegative integer and rrr i an integer with 0⩽r⩽n0 \leqslant r \leqslant n0⩽r⩽n, equals C(n,r)=n!(n−r)!r!C(n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!r!}C(n,r)=(n−r)!r!n!​
