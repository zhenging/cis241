Chapter 11 Trees

11.1 Introduction to Trees

A tree is a connected undirected graph with no simple circuits.

Theorem 1

An undirected graph is a tree if and only if there is a unique simple path between any two of its vertices.

Suppose that TTT is a rooted tree. If vvv is a vertex in TTT other than the root, the parent of vvv is the unique vertiex uuu such that there is a direct edge from uuu to vvv. When uuu is the parent of vvv, vvv is called a child of uuu. Vertices with the same parent are called siblings. The ancestors of a vertex other that the root are the vertices in the path from the root to this vertex, excluding the vertex itself and including the root (that is, its parent, its parent's parent, and so on, until the root is reached). The descendants of a vertex vvv are those vertices that have vvv as an ancestors. A vertex of a rooted tree is called a leaf if it has no children. Vertices that have children are called internal vertices. The root is an internal vertex unless it is the only vertex in the graph, in which case it is a leaf.
If aaa is a vertex in a tree, the subtree with aaa as its root is the subgraph of the tree consisting of aaa and its descendants and all edges incident to these descendants.

Theorem 2

A tree with nnn vertices has n−1n-1n−1 edges. (Proof by induction. The extra vertex must have a degree of 1, otherwise the graph would contain a cycle and would not be a valid tree.)

Theorem 3

A full m-ary tree with iii internal vertices contains n=mi+1n = mi + 1n=mi+1 vertices.

11.3 Tree Traverse

Preorder

Let TTT be an ordered rooted tree with root rrr. It TTT consists only of rrr, then rrr is the preorder trasversal of TTT. Otherwise, suppose that T1,T2,⋯TnT_1, T_2, \cdots T_nT1​,T2​,⋯Tn​ are the subtrees at rrr from left to right in TTT. The preorder trasveral begins by visiting T1T_1T1​. It continues by traversing T1T_1T1​ in preorder, then T2T_2T2​ in preorder, and so on, until TnT_nTn​ is traversed in preorder.

Root => Left => Right

Algorithem 1 Preorder traversal
procedure preorder(T: ordered rooted tree)
r := root of T
list r
for each child c of r from left to right
  T(c) := subtree with c as its root
  procedure(T(c))

Let TTT be an ordered rooted tree with root rrr. It TTT consists only of rrr, then rrr is the inorder trasversal of TTT. Otherwise, suppose that T1,T2,⋯TnT_1, T_2, \cdots T_nT1​,T2​,⋯Tn​ are the subtrees at rrr from left to right in TTT. The inorder trasveral begins by traversing T1T_1T1​ in inorder, then visiting rrr. It continues by traversing T2T_2T2​ in inorder, then T3T_3T3​ in inorder, ..., and finally TnT_nTn​ in order.

Algorithem 2 Inorder traversal
procedure inorder(T: ordered rooted tree)
r := root of T
if r is a leaf then list r
else
  l := first child of r from left to right
  T(l) := subtree with l as its root
  inorder(T(l))
  list r
  for each child c of r except l from left to right
    T(c) := subtree with c as its root
    inorder(T(c))

Let TTT be an ordered rooted tree with root rrr. It TTT consists only of rrr, then rrr is the postorder trasversal of TTT. Otherwise, suppose that T1,T2,⋯TnT_1, T_2, \cdots T_nT1​,T2​,⋯Tn​ are the subtrees at rrr from left to right in TTT. The postorder trasveral begins by traversing T1T_1T1​ in postorder, then T2T_2T2​ in postorder, ... then TnT_nTn​ in postorder, and ends by visiting rrr.
